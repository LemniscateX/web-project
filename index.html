Customs in Guangzhou

Discover some traditional customs here!

Yum Cha

Yum cha, also known as going for dim sum, is the Cantonese tradition of brunch involving Chinese tea and dim sum. The practice is popular in Cantonese-speaking regions, including Guangdong, Guangxi, Hong Kong, and Macau. It is also carried out in other regions worldwide where there are overseas Chinese communities. Yum cha generally involves small portions of steamed, pan-fried, and deep-fried dim sum dishes served in bamboo steamers, which are designed to be eaten communally and washed down with tea. People often go to yum cha in large groups for family get-togethers or celebrations.

Three Letters and six etiquettes

People in Guangzhou follow the tradition of Chinese wedding which is three letters and six etiquettes. Three letters are considered to be Betrothal Letter, Gift Letter and Wedding Letter. Six etiquettes are considered to be Proposal, Birth Date Matching, Presenting Betrothal Gifts, Presenting Wedding Gifts, Arranging The Marriage and The Wedding Ceremony. What's new in Guangzhou is that birdes usually wear a Dragon and Phoenix Skirt and grooms are expected to give a pair of Dragon and Phoenix Bracelets.

Flower fair

On Chinese New Year's Eve all around Guangzhou, there is a rush of crowds shopping for plants and flowers, before a fun evening with family and friends. It is an old custom. The origin of the flower fairs can be traced back to the ancient night flower fair. Buying flowers is so important among the Cantonese people in Guangdong and Hong Kong that there is an old saying: "No flower fairs, no Spring Festival."

Development in Guangzhou

Follow the timeline to seize the development steps!

2004

The results of the poll at the plenary session of the Doha Olympic Council of Qatar showed that Guangzhou successfully bid for the 2010 Asian Games.

2006

The 100th China Export Commodities Fair (Canton Fair) was held in Guangzhou. Wen Jiabao attended the meeting and announced that from the next (101st), China Export Commodities Fair will be officially renamed as China Import and Export Fair.

2008

The starting ceremony of the Beijing 2008 Olympic Torch Relay Guangzhou Station was held at the West Square of the Guangzhou Baiyun International Convention Center.

2010

The Bus Rapid Transit(BRT) Test Line in Zhongshan Road officially opened for operation after more than a year of construction.
The opening ceremony of the 16th Asian Games was held in Guangzhou. Chinese Premier Wen Jiabao attended and announced the opening.

2012

Guangzhou Evergrande played in the AFC Champions League for the first time in club's history after winning the league title in the 2011 season.

2016

The State Council approved the “Guangzhou City Master Plan (2011-2020)”, clarifying that Guangzhou is a national historical and cultural city, an important central city, an international business center and an integrated transportation hub in China.

2018

The Hong Kong section of the Guangzhou-Shenzhen-Hong Kong Express Rail Link, which was built in 8 years, was opened. The 141-kilometer-long Guangzhou-Shenzhen-Hong Kong high-speed railway was officially launched.
